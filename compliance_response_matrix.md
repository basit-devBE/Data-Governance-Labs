# Compliance Response Matrix

## Customer A: Abena (Ghana) – Ghana Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843)
### Legal Rights Analysis
- Abena has the right to request deletion of her personal data under Ghana Data Protection principles of data minimization and retention limitation.
- Since she has no outstanding disputes or orders, her request is generally valid.

### ShopGhana Obligations
- Verify her identity.
- Delete or anonymize personal data from operational databases.
- Retain only legally required records.

### Data That Can Be Retained
- Transaction records for tax and financial audit compliance.
- Retention period: Usually 5-7 years depending on Ghana Revenue Authority requirements.

### Response Deadline
- Must respond within a reasonable time frame (typically 30 days under best practice interpretation of Act 843).

### Action Steps + Timeline
1. Day 1-3: Verify identity and log request.
2. Day 4-14: Remove marketing and account data.
3. Day 15-30: Archive financial records securely.

### Draft Response
"Dear Abena, we have received your account deletion request. We will remove your personal data in accordance with Ghana Data Protection Act requirements while retaining legally required financial records."

## Customer B: Lukas (Germany) – GDPR
### Legal Rights Analysis
- Lukas has strong right to erasure under GDPR Article 17 since no active transactions remain.

### Exemptions
- Data may be retained for legal obligations or dispute prevention.

### ShopGhana Obligations
- Delete personal identifiers from databases.
- Notify third-party processors.
- Document compliance actions.

### Response Deadline
- 1 month (can extend by 2 months with justification).

### Penalty Risk
- Up to €20 million or 4% of global annual revenue.

### Action Steps
1. Confirm identity.
2. Remove marketing, account, and analytics data.
3. Notify partners.

### Draft Response
"Dear Lukas, your erasure request is being processed under GDPR Article 17. We will complete processing within one month."

## Customer C: Maria (USA – California) – CCPA/CPRA
### Legal Rights Analysis
- Maria has right to request deletion and to opt out of data sale.

### Constraints
- Deletion may be delayed due to active return dispute (legal defense exemption).

### ShopGhana Obligations
- Provide opt-out of sale link.
- Stop sharing her data with third parties.

### Response Deadline
- 45 days.

### Data Disclosure
- Types of data collected.
- Third parties receiving data.

### Draft Response
"Dear Maria, we acknowledge your request. We will honor your opt-out of data sale and review your deletion request, which may be delayed due to your active dispute."
